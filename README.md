## ✨ What this app does

ai-gesture-controlled-drone lets you control a drone with hand gestures. It uses your webcam or camera to read hand movements, then sends control signals to the drone. You can use simple gestures to move, stop, rise, and turn.

This project is built for people who want a hands-free control setup for a drone. It uses computer vision, TensorFlow Lite, MediaPipe, and edge AI to keep the process fast and light.

When you open the app for the first time:

1. Allow access to your camera.
2. Place yourself in front of the camera.
3. Keep your hand in view.
4. Make sure the room has clear light.

## 👋 Supported hand gestures

The app is built around common drone control gestures. These may include:

- Open hand: hover or hold
- Closed fist: stop or lock
- Two fingers: move forward
- Palm left or right: turn
- Hand up: rise
- Hand down: descend

Gesture mapping can vary by build, so check the app screen for the control guide it shows.

## 🎮 How to use it

Use the app in a clear space with your drone powered and ready.

1. Start the app.
2. Wait for the camera to detect your hand.
3. Show the gesture you want.
4. Keep the gesture steady for a short time.
5. Watch the drone response.
6. Return to a neutral hand pose when you want to pause control.

If the drone does not respond, move your hand closer to the camera and try again.

## 🧠 How it works

This app uses:

- Computer vision to find your hand in the camera feed
- MediaPipe to track hand landmarks
- A CNN model to classify gestures
- TensorFlow Lite for fast on-device inference
- Edge AI logic to keep the app responsive on local hardware

The app watches hand shape, compares it with its gesture model, then sends the matching drone command.

## 🔌 Common setup tips

Use these tips if the app has trouble reading your hand:

- Face the camera directly
- Keep your hand inside the frame
- Use even light
- Avoid shadows on your hand
- Remove items that block your fingers
- Keep the background simple
- Close other camera apps before starting this one

If the camera feed looks dark, move closer to a light source.

## 🛠️ Troubleshooting

### Camera does not open

- Check that no other app is using the camera
- Unplug and reconnect the webcam
- Restart the app
- Open Windows camera permissions and allow access

### Hand is not detected

- Hold your hand in the center of the frame
- Use brighter light
- Keep your hand open and clear
- Move closer to the camera

### Wrong gesture is detected

- Hold the gesture for a second
- Keep your fingers spread or closed in a clear shape
- Avoid fast hand movement
- Try again with a clean background

### App runs slow

- Close other apps
- Use a smaller camera resolution if the app offers it
- Reboot the PC
- Make sure your system has enough free memory
